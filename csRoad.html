_02/ Understanding the Problem

"A problem well stated is a problem half solved" _Charles Kettering

Planning The Journey

As a researcher, I wanted to understand the challenges young families can face during vacations. I wanted to understand these challenges so families could spend more quality time together, be able to travel more, and ultimately deepen their family bond.

Client Research

I created a proto personas based off of needs, roles, and average demographic information. This gave me a base of assumptions to take to the field and start validating for accuracy.

_02/ Qualitative Data

“People ignore design that ignores people.” -Frank Chimero

Interviews

I conducted five interviews with people who were representitive of the proto persona. The time of the interview varied, but most were around fifteen minutes. The stories shared by the users were a treasure trove of loves and frustrations with family road trips. We spoke about the challenges of taking the family out on the road, and some of the solutions they found along the way. Throughout the interviews a few common problems began to emerge, along with how they overcame them.

_02/ Quantitative Data

Lets talk numbers

Surveys

I noticed some patterens in the interviews, and decided to see how prevelant this pattern was. I shaped a short six question survey based on common problems and solutions, and decided to see if other road trippers agreed. The questions were put on a scale of 1-10 to add potential nuance to the data.

User Insight

I found users need to show their family how fun road trips and camping can be so they can deepen their family bond. I found all users enjoyed the ability to easily manage snacks in the car, stop at multiple destinations, and have a lot of fun outdoors at an affordable price.

The Problem

I found users get frustrated finding options upon an unforeseeable event. Events like bad weather, new ownership policies, and unavailable lodging can turn a good trip into a bad one.

The Solution

I found the more options users have access to, the more flexible they can be, and this makes the trip more enjoyable. I found having a central location that supports users every step of the way is a great resource users would appreciate.
